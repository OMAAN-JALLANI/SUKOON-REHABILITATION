Why We Started

From a frustration to a revolution.

Not Born From Profit

Five years ago, Coded Clouds didn’t start as a company — it started as a frustration. Muhammad Hashim was staring at the same problem every small business in Pakistan complained about: the undocumented economy, the cash-only chaos, the “bhai, likh diya hai” style bookkeeping that kept half the country invisible.

He wasn’t a giant corporation, he wasn’t backed by investors — just one young guy with a laptop, a vision, and that stubborn “I’m gonna fix this someday” energy.

“If hospitals are investing in management systems, why are patients still struggling with inefficiencies? We realized they didn't need another software; they needed a streamlined experience.”

Building The Foundation

So he built Coded Clouds. Not to chase hype. Not to become another copy-paste software house. The mission was simple: earn enough capital, skills, and team strength to eventually launch Pakistan’s first real fintech solution tailored for small businesses.

Every client wasn’t just a project — it was another brick in the foundation. Every late night, every deployment was part of a bigger plan: use software services to fund a solution that fixes the financial blind spot of an entire nation.
